The Framework

What Is Consent?

Real consent is built on five non-negotiable pillars. Every one must be present, every single time.

F
Freely Given

No pressure, no manipulation, no coercion. A genuine, unforced choice made without fear of consequences.

R
Reversible

Anyone can change their mind at any point - no questions asked. Respect that immediately and completely.

I
Informed

Full honesty is required. Deception, omission, or hidden intentions invalidate consent entirely.

E
Enthusiastic

Look for a clear, enthusiastic yes - not silence, hesitation, or pressure-caving.

S
Specific

Yes to one thing is not yes to everything. Each activity requires its own separate, clear consent.

Know the Truth

Myths vs. Facts

Misinformation can be harmful. Here is the truth behind common misconceptions about sex, consent, and protection.

Myth

"If they didn't say no, that means it's a yes."

Fact

Silence is never consent. Only a clear, enthusiastic "yes" counts. If someone is unsure, frozen, or quiet - stop and check in.

Myth

"Condoms ruin the experience."

Fact

Modern condoms are ultra-thin and come in many varieties. The peace of mind from being protected actually enhances the experience.

Myth

"You can tell if someone has an STI just by looking."

Fact

Most STIs show zero visible symptoms. The only way to know your status is through testing. Regular screening protects everyone.

Myth

"Consent once means consent every time."

Fact

Consent must be given every single time, for every activity. Past agreement never carries over. People can and do change their minds.

Protection Methods

Latex Barriers

Using a barrier is an act of care - for yourself and your partner. Here are the options available to you.

External Condom
Internal Condom
Free at UH Mānoa Health Services - No Questions Asked

External (Male) Condom

Up to 98% effective when used correctly. Protects against both STIs and pregnancy. Available free at campus health services - just walk in and ask.

Internal (Female) Condom

Over 95% effective. Can be inserted up to 8 hours before sex, giving the wearer more autonomy and control over their own protection.

Dental Dam

A thin latex or polyurethane sheet for oral-vaginal or oral-anal contact. Significantly reduces STI transmission risk during oral sex.

Latex / Nitrile Gloves

Used during manual stimulation to reduce STI transmission through cuts, hangnails, or direct skin contact. Simple but effective.

Practical Guidance

How to Talk About It

Not sure what to say? Here are real phrases and practices that build trust, respect, and healthier connections.

Start the Conversation

Try saying: "Before things go further, I want to make sure we're both comfortable." Opening up shows maturity, not weakness.

Check In Often

Ask: "Is this still okay?" or "Do you want to keep going?" Consent is not a one-time checkbox - it is an ongoing conversation.

Bring Up Protection

Say: "I'd feel better if we used protection - I brought some." Normalizing barrier use shows care for both of you.

Honor the Pause

"I'm not ready for that - can we slow down?" is always valid. A respectful partner will never pressure you past your boundary.

Get Tested Together

Suggest: "Let's get tested together - it's just being responsible." UH Mānoa offers free, confidential STI testing.

Be an Active Bystander

If you see someone uncomfortable at a party or gathering, step in. Check on friends. Help build a culture of genuine care.
